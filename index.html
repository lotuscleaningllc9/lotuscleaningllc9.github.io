Hard Water Stains

The water of the Tri-Cities and surrounding areas contain a substantial amount of minerals resulting in hard water for both house water and irrigation systems. Hard water staining on glass is unsightly and in many cases cause permanent damage to the glass requiring replacement of the glass. A common misconception is that spraying water onto windows and screens to rinse the dirt off is safe. When the water is sprayed onto the window the minerals in that water are left on the window and allowed to build up when the water dries. When the window is washed the dirt should be agitated with a non-scratch cleaning pad to allow the dirt to be removed effectively. However preventing scratches on the glass during the agitation process is imperative. Even small scratches that may not be visible to the naked eye will eventually start a process called hazing and hazing should be avoided. Regular household filters and water softeners still allow minerals through to the surface being cleaned. Minerals in water are what make water taste good! The minerals in the water, however if filtered or softened, will still over time start to build up and stain the glass.

Call today and scedule an appointment for a free test of the hard water removel process and experience the benefits of hard water removal.

Types of Hard Water

Different types of minerals result in different types of hard water. The minerals and chemicals in irrigation water are going to leave different types of hard water. Some hard water remedies may work more effectively on hard water stains than other remedies due to the type of minerals or chemical stains being removed. Irrigation and house water conditions vary from area to area depending on their source and handling processes. Some hard water can be removed with over the counter remedies such as Ammonia and Vinegar, however other types of hard water or layering of hard water may require an industrial acid or wax designed specifically for glass to have great results.

Hard Water Removal Process

Most windows can be restored to like-new condition. Our technicians can, at no cost, visit your location to identify the type of glass, identify the type of hard water and then provide a free on-site test spot on the water stained glass. The test spot will demonstrate how much hard water will come off with the hard water service. A common error that we normally see is that hard water stains often are left on glass by the customer for a long period of time before a professional is called. The longer the hard water is left on the glass untreated, the more likely the hard water will permanently leach into the glass, causing a stain on the glass through a process called etching.

What sets us apart from other companies is that we apply a protective wax on the glass as part of the process after the hard water stain has been removed, prolonging the value of the service. We have found that customers really appreciate having a protective barrier applied to the glass in an effort to prevent costly repairs in the future.

An estimate for hard water stain removal requires an on-site estimate. It is suggested to allow the technician to provide a free test spot on the glass in an effort to demonstrate the amount of hard water that can effectively be removed. We find customers appreciate the free test spot as it proves how effectiveness of our glass restoration process before committing to the service.

 

Solar Panel Cleaning

Cleaning solar panels is not expensive. When cleaned in a proper manner with professional equipment our customers are often surprised by how quickly the cleaning process is!

Solar panels are protected by a top layered coating of glass. If you have solar panels then you most likely have a good understanding of why it is important to keep the glass over top of the solar panels clean and as transparent as possible. Freeing the glass of dirt and other obstructions that prevent clarity will directly result in a more effective solar panel system. Washing the solar panels with equipment or chemicals that scratch or haze the surface of the glass can result in permanent damage resulting in a less efficient system. Solar panels are a large investment. Protect your investment while getting the most out of it!

A common misconception is that spraying water onto the solar panels to rinse off the dirt is effective. The dirt should be agitated with a non-scratch cleaner to be removed effectively. Preventing scratches on the glass is imperative. Even small scratches that may not be visible to the naked eye but eventually starts a process called hazing should be avoided. When rinsing the dirt off of the solar panels it is suggested also that only pure filtered water be used to prevent mineral deposit staining. Regular household filters and water softeners still allow minerals through to the surface being cleaned. Minerals in water are what make water taste good! The minerals in the water, however less than not filtered or softened, will still over time start to build up and stain.
